# Drug Legalisation & Harm Reduction Outline

## Core premise
- Legalise all psychoactive substances.
- Drug use is a high-risk activity and should be treated like other high-risk activities we already permit (motorbikes, cars, skydiving).

## Harm reduction
- Implement best-practice harm reduction strategies across the system.

## Drug licence (“drug card”)
- Prospective users must obtain a drug licence, similar to a motorcycle licence.
- Requirements:
  - Theory assessment
  - Practical assessment
  - Regular check-ins with doctors to monitor health and remain eligible

## Consumption monitoring
- An authorised government body tracks individual consumption.
- Escalating use is flagged early.
- Flagged individuals are offered support services to address drivers of escalation (e.g. mental health).

## Funding model
- Keep prices at or below current black-market levels so users switch to the legal market.
- Example: ketamine currently ~$200 ± $50 per gram (market price people already pay).
- Conservative estimate: manufacturing + hardened distribution + bureaucracy ≈ 50% of retail price.
- Remaining ~50% funds harm-reduction and support services.
- Rough trend: ~10% of users have problematic use → ~90% of consumers help fund services for those who need them.

## Pros
- Can fund mental health services for everyone, not only drug users.

## Cons
- More people will use drugs once legalised.
- Absolute number of problems will rise with that increase in use.
- Therefore: licensing, health check-ins, consumption monitoring, and support services must be sized (and funded) to absorb higher demand — not only today’s caseload.
